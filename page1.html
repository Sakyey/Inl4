A brief introduction to J.R.R Tolkien

Early Life

John Ronald Reuel Tolkien was born on January 3, 1892, in Bloemfontein, Orange Free State (now South Africa). He was the son of Arthur Reuel Tolkien and Mabel Suffield. After his father's death in 1896, Tolkien and his brother were sent to live with their mother in England. Mabel passed away when Tolkien was just 12 years old, leaving him an orphan.

Education and Career

Tolkien attended King Edward's School in Birmingham and later studied at Exeter College, Oxford. He served in World War I and was wounded in the Battle of the Somme. After the war, he became a professor of Anglo-Saxon at the University of Oxford. Tolkien is best known for his works of fantasy literature, including "The Hobbit" and "The Lord of the Rings" trilogy.

Achievements

  • Created the fictional world of Middle-earth
  • Invented several languages, including Elvish
  • Received numerous awards for his literary contributions

Best Works

  1. The Hobbit
  2. The Lord of the Rings
  3. The Silmarillion
